What are the limitations or biases in your system?
a. I would say its primary limiation is its reliance on a live API connection to Gemini 2.5 Flash. Wihtout an active internet connection or a valid API key, certain features of the agentic workflow are unavailable to the user. Additionally, the system may exhibit a species-priority bias where the AI might over-prioritize certain tasks (like "Exercise" for dogs) based on general training data rather than the specific, nuanced health requirements provided in the pet profile. The energy level logic is also limited, as it currently uses a simple 1–3 integer scale as a proxy for complex human physical constraints
Could your AI be misused, and how would you prevent that?
a. A significant risk of misuse would be an owner treating the AI as a veterinary diagnostic tool rather than a logistical assistant. To prevent this, the application is architected to handle task scheduling and time management rather than providing medical advice. The code includes "Guardrails" in the form of a Verification Phase, which checks the AI's suggested schedule against the owner's actual time budget to ensure the plan is physically grounded in reality, not just a hallucinated suggestion.
What surprised you while testing your AI's reliability?
a.During testing, it was surprising how often the AI added "conversational fluff" (like markdown backticks or introductory text) to its JSON responses. This behavior caused the standard json.loads() function to fail with a char 0 error until I implemented a JSON cleaning helper to strip away non-data characters.
Describe your collaboration with AI during this project. Identify one instance when the AI gave a helpful suggestion and one instance where its suggestion was flawed or incorrect.                                                    
a. One instance of a helpful suggestion was when The AI suggested the Agentic Workflow in a Plan-Execute-Verify format, which transformed the scheduler from a simple sorting list into a "reasoning engine" that could refine its own plans based on owner constraints.
One instance of a flawed suggestion was when the AI provided a code block that placed a from __future__ import after several other import statements. This led to a syntax error because Python requires these specific imports to appear at the absolute beginning of the file.
